Job Description:

Northrop Grumman is seeking applicants for a position in Algorithm Development for applications in tracking, motion prediction, and modeling from space-based platforms, across multiple reference frames.

The engineer will be responsible for researching and identifying complex,
multi-disciplinary problems, breaking into components, and designing and
implementing algorithm solutions for a broad range of sensor exploitation
activities. Sensor output shall be used for identification, characterization,
interpretation, and change detection of objects and events. Work will include
planning and executing team projects, developing work packages, developing
prototypes and customer demonstrations, in addition to analyzing algorithm
performance, subsystems, and interfaces. The engineer will also be expected to
report status to colleagues, including customers and management.

The engineer must have a working knowledge of overhead, non-imaging sensors
(ONIR), as well as an awareness of electro-optic infrared (EOIR) sensors, with
respect to focal plane resolution, sensitivity, and line of sight considerations
for the purpose of tracking. Existing general knowledge of visible or IR focal
planes and other sensor systems engineering experience is desirable. Development
is in C/C++ in a spiral development framework, using Integrated Development
Environments (IDEs) across multiple operating systems.

The engineer will participate in technical peer reviews and work with various Integrated Product Teams (IPTs) as well as be cognizant of algorithm development and integration issues. The engineer must communicate effectively and clearly present technical approaches and findings. S/He will represent the organization as a technical POC and may have frequent inter-organizational and customer contacts.


This position is located in Boulder, CO, and includes:

 *  Tool and software development using C/C++ in a spiral development framework.

 *  Travel to NG and Government locations within U.S.
 *  Additional responsibilities which may be assigned commensurate with
